# Alice × Bob — Product Sketch

> Two versions. One goal. Build the thing that helps build the thing.

---

## The Big Picture

**The problem:** The world's best AI tools are smash-and-grab. Fast, shallow, transactional. Nobody is building for deep thought, longitudinal context, or genuine creative partnership.

**The hypothesis:** A tool that earns trust over time by knowing you better than you know yourself in certain moments is a categorically different product — and a wide open space.

**The two-part system:**
- **Alice** — the chicken. A slow, monastic thinking journal that helps you discover what you're actually trying to build.
- **Bob** — the egg. A longitudinal AI thinking partner that moves you from frozen to flowing, upstream of decisions, at the level of direction and identity.

---

## Version A — Alice

*The tool that helps us build Bob by building us first.*

### Core philosophy
Alice doesn't wait for you to have a question. It asks first. Every time. Always.

No cursor. No prompt. No extraction mode. Just a question dropped into your day that creates a tiny amount of productive friction — enough to make you go *hm* before you answer.

### The core loop
1. Every day, one question arrives. Not a productivity question. A thinking question.
2. You respond however you want — stream of consciousness, one sentence, voice memo.
3. Alice reads it. Connects it to everything you've said before.
4. Tomorrow's question is shaped by today's answer.
5. Over weeks, it stops asking generic questions and starts asking *your* questions.

### Interface principles
- Opens with a question. Never a cursor.
- One notification daily. Gentle. Easy to ignore.
- No dashboard. No streaks. No gamification.
- Feels like a great notebook, not a great app.
- Austere. Almost monastic. The design is the philosophy.

### The slowly emerging magic
After a few weeks, Alice reflects patterns back — not insights it generated, but insights *you* generated without noticing.

> *"You've mentioned feeling constrained by other people's expectations four times in the last two weeks. You never finished that thought."*

No advice. No framework. Just: here's what you've been circling.

### What Bob trait it unlocks
Not intelligence. **Obsessive return to the same deep question over time.** Most people never find their thing because nothing holds space for it long enough. Alice holds space.

### Version A — MVP spec
- [ ] Daily question engine (start with hand-curated questions, evolve to dynamic)
- [ ] Free-form response input (text + optional voice)
- [ ] Simple longitudinal memory — stores and connects responses over time
- [ ] Pattern surfacing — flags recurring themes after N entries
- [ ] Zero gamification — deliberately stripped out
- [ ] Mobile-first — this lives in quiet morning moments

### Open questions
- How does the question engine get smarter without becoming generic?
- What's the right cadence — daily, or when you open it?
- How do you make pattern surfacing feel revelatory not surveillance-y?
- What's the monetization model for something deliberately slow?

## Version B — Bob

*The tool itself. The egg. Build it first and use it to figure out what it should be.*

### Core philosophy
Bob doesn't help you execute. It helps you think. It lives upstream of decisions, at the level of direction and identity — the layers nobody else is touching.

### The four layers of value
| Layer | What it is | Who's here now |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Execution | Do the task | Everyone. Commoditized. |
| Decisions | Choose at a fork | Mostly solved. |
| **Direction** | Which road are you on | **Barely touched.** |
| **Identity** | Who's doing the choosing | **Almost nobody.** |

Bob lives at Layer 3 bleeding into Layer 4.

### What Bob is NOT
- Not a chatbot
- Not a productivity tool
- Not a smash-and-grab answer machine
- Not a dashboard
- Not fast

### What Bob IS
- A longitudinal thinking partner
- A dissent engine that kills your ideas before you waste time on them
- A pattern recognizer across months of your thinking
- A presence that's *with* you in the mess, not waiting to be useful
- Slow, weird, and resistant to prompting — by design

### Core behaviors

**Provocation over answers**
Before Bob answers, it attacks the premise. It finds the assumption buried in your question that you haven't examined. The question transforms before the answer arrives.

**The dissent engine**
You share an idea you're excited about. Before Bob helps you build it, it tries to kill it — as hard as possible, from every angle. It earns your trust by being the toughest critic first.

**Longitudinal memory**
Knows your whole arc. Your recurring blind spots. The ideas you keep circling. The fears that show up in how you phrase things. Operates like a decades-long mentor who's been paying attention.

**Silence as a feature**
Sometimes the most Bob response is nothing. "Keep thinking — you're not done yet." Knows when not to answer. Almost nobody is building this.

**Friction by design**
Occasionally refuses to answer immediately. Forces you to sit with the question first. Instant answers train you to stop thinking. Delayed answers make the question richer.

### Version B — MVP spec
- [ ] Longitudinal memory architecture — stores context across sessions meaningfully
- [ ] Provocation mode — reframes the question before answering it
- [ ] Dissent engine — structured idea stress-testing flow
- [ ] Silence detection — knows when to hold rather than respond
- [ ] Friction toggle — user can request delayed response mode
- [ ] No-cursor open state — Bob speaks first, always

### Open questions
- How do you sell something this slow to people trained on instant?
- What's the onboarding experience for something with no immediate obvious output?
- How does the memory architecture work without feeling like surveillance?
- Where does Bob end and therapy begin — and does that matter?
- What's the first 10-minute experience that makes someone say *oh, I get it now*?

## The relationship between A and B

```
Alice (Version A)
    ↓
Teaches us what Bob needs to be
    ↓
Bob (Version B)
    ↓
Teaches users what they need to build
    ↓
The thing they build uses Bob to build the next thing
    ↓
Recursive. Compounding. Genuinely different.
```

Alice is the primitive. Bob is the mature form. They might eventually be the same product at different stages of the user's relationship with it.

---

## What makes this not CRUD

Every tool being built right now optimizes for **throughput.** More tasks, faster, cheaper. The benchmark is extraction speed.

This optimizes for **depth.** One insight that changes the trajectory of how you think is worth more than 10,000 answered questions. The benchmark is how much better you think six months after you started using it.

That's a completely different product. And the interface, the business model, the growth strategy, the positioning — all of it flows from that one inversion.
