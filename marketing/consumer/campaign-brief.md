# Consumer (DTC) Campaign Brief

## Market Context

The consumer journaling/wellness app market is saturated (20,000+ mental health apps) and experiencing backlash. Users cycle through apps every 2-3 months. 77% of users abandon apps within three days. The dominant retention mechanic (streaks) is now itself a source of anxiety. Public sentiment on Reddit, Product Hunt, and app store reviews reveals a growing population of "wellness app refugees" who have tried Calm, Headspace, Reflectly, Daylio, and Rosebud and abandoned all of them.

These are not non-journalers. They are lapsed journalers. They want the practice. They reject the packaging.

## Target Persona

**The Lapsed Practitioner**
- 28-45, has tried 3+ journaling or wellness apps
- Abandoned them after the prompts got repetitive, the streaks created guilt, or the AI felt hollow
- Reads about depth, contemplation, or philosophy (Stoicism, Buddhism, existentialism, phenomenology)
- Distrusts "wellness" branding
- Skeptical of AI but open to AI that serves a clear purpose
- Values privacy instinctively
- Not looking for therapy; looking for thinking practice

**Secondary: The Never-Started**
- Wants to journal but finds blank pages paralyzing and prompt lists juvenile
- Has bought a Moleskine, written in it twice, and feels guilty about it
- Would respond to a single, well-chosen question better than a blank page or a menu

## Value Proposition

The journal that doesn't perform for you. One question per day, generated from your accumulated responses. No streaks. No mood graphs. No AI telling you what you feel. Just a question that gets sharper the longer you stay.

## Key Messages

### Primary
**"One question. Every day. No score."**
Positions immediately against the entire category. Three constraints. Zero engagement mechanics.

### Supporting Messages

**"Your answers aren't for you to review. They're for better questions tomorrow."**
Explains the black box without calling it a black box. Reframes the absence of data surfacing as a feature of the question engine.

**"The journal that forgets so you don't have to."**
Emotional hook. Addresses the anxiety of re-reading old entries. Positions the black box as relief, not deprivation.

**"91% of self-trackers report tracking-related stress. We track nothing you can see."**
Evidence-based. Directly attacks the dashboard paradigm. The stat is real (2025 survey, n=2,999).

**"Depth, not data."**
Two-word category rejection.

### vs. Rosebud ("The AI one")

## Objection Handling

**"I want to look back at what I wrote."**
The desire to review past entries is natural but counterproductive for most journalers. Re-reading old entries often triggers rumination, not growth. Alice's design is based on the research: what you wrote matters because it shapes tomorrow's question, not because you revisit it.

**"How do I know it's working if I can't see data?"**
You know it's working because the questions get harder to dismiss. The quality of the question is the only feedback signal. If you find yourself thinking about a question hours after you answered it, it's working.

**"One question per day isn't enough."**
The constraint is the practice. Three pages of freeform writing is Morning Pages. A conversation with an AI is therapy simulation. One question that was generated specifically for you, from your history, is something else. The depth comes from the constraint, not despite it.

**"I don't trust AI with my journal."**
Alice's AI reads your responses to generate tomorrow's question. It does not converse, comment, analyze for you, or share. The signal engine is local-first. There is no dashboard for anyone to access, including you.

## Channel Strategy

### Organic
- Long-form essays on the failure modes of wellness apps (Medium, Substack, personal blog)
- Reddit presence in r/journaling, r/digitalminimalism, r/stoicism, r/meditation (not promotional; contribute to discussions about depth, blank-page anxiety, app fatigue)
- Product Hunt launch positioned as anti-category: "Not a wellness app"

### Paid (Phase 2)
- Podcast sponsorship on philosophy/depth podcasts (not productivity or self-help)
- Targeted to users who have recently uninstalled Calm, Headspace, or Reflectly (programmatic)
- App Store search ads on "journaling app no streaks," "minimalist journal," "private journal app"

### Earned
- Pitch to writers covering contemplative technology, slow tech, attention economy
- Academic partnerships producing research on Alice's signal data (publications become credibility anchors)

## Success Metrics (Internal Only)

Do not surface these to users. These are organizational health metrics.

- **Response depth** (word count, lexical diversity, semantic coherence per response)
- **Return rate without prompting** (users who return without push notifications)
- **Longevity** (median days-of-use before permanent churn)
- **Question quality** (internal NLP evaluation of question relevance to response history)

We do not measure DAU, streaks, or session time. Those metrics optimize for the wrong thing.
