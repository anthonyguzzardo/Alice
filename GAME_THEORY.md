# Game Theory: Alice at Scale

Hypothetical scenarios explored 2026-04-18. Filed under "things worth thinking about even if they never happen."

## The Information Advantage

Alice captures two data streams no existing system combines:

1. Pre-linguistic motor signal (keystroke dynamics, revision behavior, pause structure) that the user cannot consciously manipulate
2. Longitudinal behavioral biometrics under controlled conditions (same interface, same constraints, daily calibration baseline)

An LLM with two years of chat history has more data. Alice with two years of daily entries has more information. The distinction: data is volume, information is signal with structure. Chat history models what someone wants to say. Alice measures how someone is while they say it.

Game-theoretically, Alice holds a dominant information advantage because the signal channel cannot be corrupted by strategic behavior. The user cannot perform for an instrument that reads involuntary motor timing.

## Why This Doesn't Exist

- Medicine is reactive. No infrastructure for continuous pre-symptomatic cognitive monitoring.
- The wrong people are building. Academic labs publish papers, not products. Product companies optimize for engagement, not measurement.
- Consent is a minefield. A keystroke logger one UX decision away from spyware. Alice sidesteps this by being single-user, local-first, no company, no harvest.
- Depth doesn't scale commercially. One question per day, no gamification, no social. No VC funds deliberate engagement limitation.
- Research is siloed. Writing process, digital phenotyping, cognitive reserve, and keystroke biometrics are all measuring the same thing from different angles. Nobody has operationalized the convergence.

Alice exists because it was built for one person without needing to be a business. That is the only context where this design survives without compromise.

## n=1 vs n>1

The n=1 version is the purest form. Every step toward n>1 introduces forces that erode the design:

- Consent frameworks and data governance
- Clinical validation and regulatory burden if making health claims
- The temptation to add streaks, badges, dashboards
- Explaining why a typing instrument matters more than a wearable

There is a middle path: n=100 to n=1000 without becoming a product. Research cohorts. Clinical partnerships. A neurologist who wants longitudinal keystroke data on early-stage patients. That path requires one credible study with a meaningful outcome, not growth hacking.

## The Chicago Simulation

2.7 million people. 365 days. One question each. Nearly a billion journal entries with full keystroke biometrics.

### What emerges

A population-level cognitive weather map. Real-time motor and linguistic baselines for an entire city. This has never existed.

### Predictive public health

Epidemiology currently counts sick people after they are sick. A city-scale cognitive baseline would show population stress, cognitive load, and neurological drift in near real-time. Mental health crises visible weeks before emergency rooms fill. Resources allocated to neighborhoods showing collective cognitive strain before anyone calls a hotline.

### Neurology gets a screening tool

There is no annual cognitive checkup. No blood pressure equivalent for the brain. Cost and compliance prevent neuropsych evaluations at population scale. Ten minutes of daily writing with passive motor channel screening changes that equation. Early Parkinson's, Alzheimer's, MS, depression onset flagged years before clinical presentation. Routed to care while intervention still works.

### Education gets honest feedback

Students showing declining deliberation depth and increasing revision avoidance over a semester. That is not a test score. That is real-time signal about cognitive development. Curriculum changes evaluated at the process level, not the output level.

### Urban planning gets a new input

Noise pollution, air quality, commute stress affect cognition. A neighborhood's collective motor variance spiking after a highway expansion is evidence that was previously anecdotal. Policy grounded in involuntary biological response, not surveys filled out carelessly.

### The net positive

Close the gap between when something goes wrong in a population and when anyone notices. That gap is where most preventable damage lives.

## The Enemy-Friend Paradox

The same properties that make this scientifically extraordinary make it ethically unprecedented. Pre-linguistic cognitive state, daily, involuntary, for millions. Keystroke dynamics are biometrics closer to fingerprints than survey responses.

But the data already exists. Every keystroke typed on any device is processed by software the user did not write, governed by terms they did not read, owned by companies whose incentive is monetization. BioCatch knows your motor signature better than you do. Your bank uses it to verify your identity. You have never seen it.

The asymmetry is not whether the data exists. It is whether you have access to what it says about you.

Alice inverts the orientation. Same data, pointed inward instead of outward. Not being watched. Watching yourself. Awareness chosen, not imposed.

That is not surveillance. That is sovereignty.

## Utilitarian / Moral Relativist / Game Theory Assessment

**Utilitarian:** Alice's controlled longitudinal data can detect pre-symptomatic cognitive decline. An LLM's scattered chat history can refine preference prediction. The utility ceiling of early neurological detection versus better autocomplete is not comparable.

**Moral relativist:** Data gathered with explicit awareness and intent (Alice's contract) is ethically cleaner than data harvested as a byproduct of convenience (chat history profiling) under any cultural or ethical framework. Informed consent changes the moral calculus entirely.

**Game theory:** Alice is a repeated game with a single cooperating partner under fixed rules where the user cannot defect because the signal is involuntary. An LLM conversation is a repeated game where the user constantly shifts strategy: serious, lazy, testing, pasting someone else's words. The LLM cannot distinguish between the player and the noise. Alice always knows who is at the keyboard because the keyboard is the signal.

## The Outlook

Optimistic about the science. The signal is real, replicated, grounded.

Skeptical about the delivery. Every force that prevented this from existing activates at n>1.

Uncertain about the middle path. One credible study could change everything. Or nothing.

The telescope works. Whether it becomes an observatory program or stays in the backyard depends on choices that have not been made yet and do not need to be.
